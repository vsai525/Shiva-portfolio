## ☕ First, what are we actually doing?

Don't worry about the words yet. Here's the whole plan in plain English:

1. We'll use a **free AI assistant** (Claude or Codex) that can *write code for
   you* when you just **talk to it in normal English**.
2. We'll grab my **ready-made website** as a starting point (it's free and
   public).
3. The AI will help you **change the words, photos, and colors** to make it
   *yours* — you just tell it what you want.
4. We'll put it **online for free** so anyone in the world can visit it.
5. We'll even get you a **free web address** (like `yourname.com`-ish).

You will **not** need to understand the code. You just need to **read, click,
copy, paste, and talk** to the AI. That's it. Promise. 🤝

## 🪜 The Step-by-Step

### Step 1 — Make a free GitHub account

**What is GitHub?** Think of it as **Google Drive, but for websites and code.**
It's where your website's files live safely online. It's free.

1. Go to **https://github.com**
2. Click **Sign up**
3. Enter your email, pick a username (this can be your name, like `johnsmith`),
   and a password.
4. Verify your email when they send you a code.

✅ That's it. You now have a GitHub account. Keep your username and password
somewhere safe.

---

### Step 2 — Get your AI helper (Claude **or** Codex)

This is the magic part. An AI that **writes the code for you** while you just
chat. You only need **one** of these — pick whichever you like.

**Option A — Claude (this is what I used, highly recommend) 💜**

1. Go to **https://claude.ai**
2. Sign up for a free account (use your email or Google login).
3. To let it actually build websites on your computer, you'll want
   **"Claude Code"** — it's the version that can create and edit files for you.
   Search "Claude Code download" or visit
   **https://www.anthropic.com** and follow the "Claude Code" setup.

**Option B — Codex (by OpenAI)**

1. Go to **https://chatgpt.com** and sign up.
2. Look for **"Codex"** in their tools — it's their version of an AI that
   writes and edits code for you.

> 🙋 **"Which one should I pick?"** Honestly, either works. I used **Claude**
> and loved it. If you're unsure, start with Claude.

> 💡 Both have **free tiers**. You may hit usage limits if you do a *lot* in one
> day — just wait a bit and continue, or it resets. You don't *need* to pay to
> build your portfolio.

---

### Step 3 — Connect your AI to GitHub

Remember: GitHub is the storage, the AI is the builder. We want them to talk to
each other so the AI can save your website to your GitHub.

The good news: **you don't do this manually.** You literally just **ask the AI**.

Type something like this to your AI:

> *"I want to connect you to my GitHub account so you can save my website
> there. Walk me through it step by step, I'm not technical."*

The AI will then guide you — usually it asks you to click a button to
"authorize" or "log in to GitHub," and you just say **yes / allow**. That's the
whole connection. 🔗

---

### Step 4 — Grab my ready-made portfolio

Now tell your AI to use my website as your starting point. Copy-paste this to
your AI:

> *"Please download (clone) this GitHub project so we can use it as a starting
> point for my own portfolio:
> https://github.com/Deepakvutla9/deepak-portfolio
> Then set it up and run it so I can see it on my computer. I'm not technical,
> so explain what's happening in simple words."*

The AI will fetch the files and get the website running. In a minute or two it
will give you a link like **http://localhost:3000** — open that in your web
browser and 🎉 **you'll see the website running on your own computer!**

> 📌 `localhost:3000` just means *"this website, but only on your computer for
> now."* The world can't see it yet — we'll fix that in Step 6.

---

### Step 5 — Make it YOURS (the fun part 🎨)

This is where you don't touch any code — you just **talk**. Tell the AI what you
want changed, in plain English. Here are real examples you can copy and tweak:

- *"Change the name everywhere from Deepak to **[Your Name]**."*
- *"Change the headline to: **Building Brands That People Trust.**"*
- *"Replace the projects with my real work: I'm a **[your job, e.g. wedding
  photographer]**. Here are my 4 best projects: ..."*
- *"Change the colors to a warm gold and cream theme instead of purple."*
- *"Update the About section — here's my story: ..."*
- *"Put my email as **myname@email.com** and my Instagram link as ..."*
- *"Remove the music player, I don't want background music."*

After each request, **refresh your `localhost:3000` page** to see the change.
Don't like it? Just say *"actually, change it back"* or *"make it bigger."* The
AI doesn't get tired. Experiment freely. 😄

> ✨ **Tip:** Go section by section. Do the name first, then the headline, then
> About, then projects. Small steps = less confusion.

---

### Step 6 — Put it online for the world (free!) 🌍

Right now only you can see it. Let's publish it so you can share the link with
anyone — friends, recruiters, clients, your mom. We'll use **Vercel**, which
hosts websites like this **for free**.

Just tell your AI:

> *"I'm happy with my website. Please deploy it to Vercel for free and give me a
> public link I can share. Walk me through any sign-in steps."*

The AI will:
1. Send your website to **Vercel** (sign up free with your GitHub account —
   one click).
2. Give you a **live web address** like
   **`your-name.vercel.app`** 🎉

**That link is now LIVE on the internet.** Share it anywhere. Anyone can visit.

> 🔄 **Bonus:** Every time you change something later and "save it to GitHub,"
> your live website updates **automatically.** No extra work.

---

### Step 7 — Get a free custom web address (optional but classy 😎)

`your-name.vercel.app` is great. But maybe you want something cleaner like
`yourname.com`-style. Here's how to do it **for free**:

**Free options:**
- **Freenom-style / free domain providers** — search *"free domain name"*
  (some offer free `.com`-like addresses, though availability changes).
- **`is-a.dev`** — a community project giving free `yourname.is-a.dev` addresses
  to developers and creators. Free and reliable.
- **GitHub Student Pack** — if you're a student, you get a **free `.me` domain
  for a year** (from Namecheap) plus tons of other free goodies. Sign up at
  **https://education.github.com/pack** with your school email.

Once you have a free domain, just tell your AI:

> *"I got a free domain called **[your-domain]**. Help me connect it to my
> Vercel website, step by step."*

It'll walk you through copying a couple of settings. Takes 5 minutes.

## 🆘 If you get stuck

The single most powerful trick in this whole guide:

> **When something doesn't work, copy the error or describe what happened, paste
> it to your AI, and say: "This isn't working, I'm not technical, help me fix
> it."**

The AI is your patient, never-annoyed teacher. There's no "dumb question." I
asked mine a *hundred* basic things. That's the whole point. 💪
